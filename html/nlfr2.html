1 Why would you like to learn English?

1.1 In order to make your brain bigger

According to a Swedish study (you can watch this video as well), learning a new language will make your brain bigger. And it could be also the best way to prevent Alzeihmer disease (the link is here).

So obviously it's not specific to English but if you want to study any language I strongly recommend you to improve your English level first, otherwise you will not have access to this for example.

Whatever the topic you would like to study, it would be very easier to find acurate information in English. All the best expert in every field publish in English, all the best books are written or translated in English, almost everything is more documented in English. Just look at the number of Wikipedia articles in English compare to any other languages.

1.2 In order to have access to more culture

Did you know that during the 18th century French was the lingua Franca? But nowadays English is really more important in every field. The good news is that if you already know French, knowing English will give you access to the best of the two worlds. And by the way 29% of English words have French origins and 29% have latin origins. So it shouldn't be difficult to get the vocabulary.

Anyway, as I said before in evry field you'll find more documentation in English. So what are you waiting for?

1.3 In order to be more confident

Learning English is like learning how to perform a role. As I always say to my student, learning English is like acting. English is a language of theater or movies. It's definitively a speaking language where sounds are really very important. The main problem for French speaker (and for people who get trouble with English) is to not pay enough attention to the oral traps. English is not an easy language. Actually it's a fake easy language. It looks like an easy language (particularly when you're a French speaker or a Romance language speaker) because of the latin-base vocabulary. But the most difficult part (and the most important) is the oral part.

If you want to improve your oral skills you can read at loud english audiobook like this one for example.

1.4 Add your personal reason here

Obviously you can find tons of reasons to learn English. But never forget that learning a new language will be efficient only if you practice it regularly and that means that you have to have good motivation. I recommend you this website if you want to learn how to build good zen habits.

4 Tips of the week

4.1 Become more natural with the language

The best way to sound natural is to talk with natives. Obviously you cannot find natives everywhere you live… But, thanks to internet and apps, yes wen can! One of my favorites apps to do that is tandem. I strongly recommend you to use it. It's a kind of social network for language learners.

4.2 Be aware of grammar rules

If you like to learn grammar tips go to this blog: https://www.englishgrammar.org/. The author provides tons of lessons and exercices. That's a good place for learning but remember that books are the best way to improve your writing skills.

5 Games of the week

Since last newsletter I've decided to add an heading: Games of the week. Because games are a fun way to learn.

5.1 The mystery word

5.1.1 Level 1

Try to guess in maximum 7 attempts the word that is written with 4 different letters and that is the basis of vocabulary.

5.1.2 Level 2

Try to guess in maximum 7 attemps the word that is written with 7 letters but only 3 different consonant and 1 vowel. Three letters are used twice and only one letter is used once.

Hint: for some people that's the heart of a language.
