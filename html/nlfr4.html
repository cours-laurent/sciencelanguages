2.2 Grammar tips and tricks

2.2.1 Parts of the speech

Let's look (or review) the word types:

  1. Noun

    A word used to name a person, animal, place, or thing. Here is a tip to find them in a sentence: "ask the question who is the subject of the sentence?"

    Examples:

    a person
    Laurent is a proper noun, so it starts with a capital letter even if it's not in the beginning of the sentence like in: "My name is Laurent."
    an animal
    People often say that the cat is the best friend of the woman. "Cat" is the name of an animal, but it's a common name so it doesn't take a capital letter unless it's the beginning of the sentence.
    a place
    Moscow is the capital of Russia. Here we have 3 nouns, 2 proper nouns (Moscow and Russia) and 1 common noun (capital). They are all names of places.
    a thing
    My computer is my best tool. Here we have 2 common nouns (computer and tool).
  2. Adjective

    A word used to describe a noun or pronoun. Here is a trick to find them in a sentence: ask the question "how is the noun?"

    Examples:

    French
    Laurent is a French name. "French" is an adjective because it describes the proper name (Laurent) by adding information to the sentence.
    best
    The cat is the best friend of the woman. "Best" is an adjective because it describes the (common) name "cat"; it's not a normal friend that's a special one.
    cold
    Russia is a cold country, especially in winter. "Cold" is an adjective because it describes the (common) noun "country".
    old
    My computer is an old one because I've bought it in 2010. "Old" is an adjective because it describes the (common) noun "computer".
  3. Verb

    A word that expresses an action or a state of being. Here is a tip to find them in a sentence: ask the question "what's the action?"

    Examples:

    to be
    Laurent is a person. "Is" is the verb to be that expresses the existence of Laurent as a person (human being).
    to jump
    The cat jumps out of the couch. "Jumps" is the the verb to jump that expresses the motion of the cat.
    to belong
    Russia belongs to Europe and Asia because of its size. "Belongs" is the verb to belong that expresses a state of being a part of a group.
    to compute
    My computer computes calculus faster than me. "Computes" is the verb to compute that expresses the action of doing calculus.
  4. Adverb

    A word that describes and adds information to a verb or a verb phrase. The adverb is to the verb what the adjective is to the noun. Here is a trick to find them: they often end with "ly" (but not always).

    Examples:

    easily
    Laurent is easily recognizable as a French name because of its pronunciation. "Easily" is an adverb related to the verb "is". It describes how Laurent is recognizable as a French name.
    quickly
    The cat jumps quickly when the dog arrives. "Quickly" is referring to "jumps".
    particularly
    Moscow is particularly crowdy because it's the capital. "Particularly" refers to "is".
    slowly
    When I make videos my computer works slowly. "Slowly" is referring to "works".
  5. Pronoun

    A word that takes the place of a noun. Actually, that's a kind of alias. It's a shorter way to refer to the noun.

    Advice: Use it only after having introduced the name. Because the listener (or reader) has to know the reference if you want her/him to understand.

    Examples:

    he
    /Laurent is a great teacher. You just say that because he offers you free tips and tricks in his newsletter./ "He" is a pronoun that is a reference to the (proper) noun "Laurent".
    it
    A cat is a pet. It is not a person. "It" is a pronoun referring to the (common) noun "The cat".
    she
    Sarah is a writer, she has written a lot of books. "She" is a pronoun referring to the (proper) noun "Sarah".
  6. Preposition

    A word that indicates the relationship between two people or things, usually in terms of where they are. Preposition means "place before". So it is usually before the noun phrase.

    Examples:

    on
    Laurent is on his computer when he types the newsletters. "On" is a preposition that is before the noun phrase "his computer".
    in
    The cat spend most of his time in the house. "In" is a preposition placed before the noun phrase "the house".
    down
    Moscow is down St Petersburg. "Down" is a preposition that indicates the position of Moscow relatively to St Petersburg.
    at
    My computer is at home when I'm going outside. "At" is a preposition that is before the noun "home".
  7. Conjunction

    A word used to link words and clauses. We can call that a connector or a linking word.

    Examples:

    but
    Laurent is a French name but with Latin root so we can find derivatived names in other languages. "But" and "so" are conjunctions.
    and
    The cat is the best friend of the woman and the dog is the best friend of the man. "And" is a conjunction.
    because
    Moscow is a famous city because it's a capital but St Petersburg used to be a capital as well. "Because" and "but" are conjunctions.
    yet
    My computer is old, yet it still works for the tasks I need to do. "Yet" is a conjunction here (be careful because yet can also have an adverb function).
  8. Interjection

    A word that usually occurs alone and expresses emotion.

    Examples:

    oh
    Oh, that's surprising to see you this morning.
    ah
    Ah, I'm happy to see you again.
    ouch
    Ouch, I haven't seen that the door is low.
    phew
    Phew, thanks to you we avoid this crazy driver.
    yuck
    Yuck, I don't like ketchup, that's disgust me.
    hooray
    Hooray, we win the game.
    help
    Help, I need somebody help… (listen to the Beatles song).
    er
    Er, frankly speaking I don't know the name of the currency in Venezuela.
    um
    Um, it's hard to choose because all the things in that menu looks very good.
    oops
    Oops, I forgot to wish you a happy birthday yesterday, I'm sorry.
  9. Article

    A word used with a noun to refer to a specific person or thing, or someone or something in general.

    Examples:

    a
    Laurent is a French name. "a" is the article of the phrase noun "French name".
    an
    The cat is an animal. "an" is an article.
    the
    Moscow is the capital of Russia. "The" is an article.
  10. Determiner

    A word used in front of a noun to denote something specific or something of a particular type. Articles are also determiners.

    Examples:

    those
    Those things over there. "*Those" determines the specific things that we talk about.
    many
    There are many reasons to learn a new language. "Many" determines the number of reasons.
    my
    The computer I use to write this newsletter is my computer. "My" determines that it's not a random computer, that's the one I've bought.
    his
    Laurent is French so his native language is French. "His" determines the native language that Laurent speaks.
    (no term)
    few : May I ask you few questions? "Few" determines the number of questions.
  11. Summary example

    Let's have a look at a sentence that uses all parts of the speech.

    A computer quickly completes its long tasks but in a way that doesn't involve initiative.

    adjective adverb article conjunction determiner noun preposition pronoun verb
    long quickly a but that computer in its complete
              tasks     doesn't
              way     involve
              initiative
